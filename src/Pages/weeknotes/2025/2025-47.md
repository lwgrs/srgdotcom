### Things this week 
This week started with my last scheduled day off for a while. I have three days left to book, but I'm not sure when I'm taking them. Still, it was nice - I felt more "productive", and I didn't feel like I spent too much time doing any one thing. The best thing about my day off was the pleasant surprise that I wasn't dragging my feet to work Tuesday morning and didn't hit an afternoon wall. 

Wednesday though...woof. It seemed like every time I went to put my headphones in to listen to a podcast, I would be interrupted minutes later. I'd take them out to interact with whomever needed my attention, and left them out. An extended period of time would elapse and I thought it safe to put them back in...only to be interrupted again minutes later. Must be some sort of rule or law about that. 

Our condo corporation hired contractors to replace the siding on the units, and there's now scaffolding set up at both our front and back doors. Going out the front is kind of nice but needs more decoration. Would they be upset if we hung Christmas lights? 
### Health Notes
I got my blood work results back (the bruise on my arm is turning some funny colours a week later) and turns out my cholesterol levels are high, and higher than the last round of blood work. Something I need to work on. 

We also received our COVID and flu shots this weekend. I had some flu-like symptoms on Saturday, which is normal, but a first for me.
